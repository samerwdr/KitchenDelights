Yabraq (Syrian Stuffed Grape Leaves)

Preparing fresh ingredients for the stuffing
Wrapping the rice and meat mixture in grape leaves
Cooking the stuffed grape leaves
Simmering the grape leaves until tender
Finished Yabraq served with lemon and fresh herbs

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice, washed and drained
  • 300 g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp allspice
  • ½ tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • 500 g grape leaves (fresh or jarred)
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 2 cups water or light broth

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the grape leaves by rinsing jarred leaves or blanching fresh leaves for 2–3 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix rice, minced meat, onion, olive oil, salt, pepper, allspice, and cinnamon.
  3. Place a grape leaf shiny side down, add 1 tsp stuffing near the stem, fold sides, and roll tightly.
  4. Line the bottom of a pot with grape leaves and garlic slices, then arrange rolls tightly in layers.
  5. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice over the rolls, add water or broth until just covering.
  6. Place a heavy plate on top and simmer on low heat for 45-60 minutes until rice is cooked.
  7. Let rest 10 minutes before serving. Serve warm with yogurt.

Serving Tips: Traditionally served with yogurt; tastes better the next day; can be appetizer or main dish.
